Rotating SLF Trap

Explore the Project
01

Problem Statement

Spotted lanternfly (SLF) infestations are not effectively controlled by current methods, which are labor-intensive, inconsistent, and disruptive to farming operations. SLF significantly reduces grape yield and harms agricultural ecosystems, leading to economic losses and increased strain on farmers.

02

Design Goal

Design and prototype a low-cost rotating trap that passively attracts spotted lanternflies using Tree-of-Heaven sap and mechanically directs them into a contained collection chamber. The system should be easy to manufacture and operate with minimal maintenance in vineyard environments.

03

Success Criteria + Test Methods

①

Peg Guidance Test

Assesses: Reaper wall & peg plate guides marble to center without jamming, escaping, or compressing

GPI = 100(St − 0.4Bt − 0.7Jt − 1.0Ct)
②

Chamber Drop Test

Assesses: Marble falls cleanly into collection chamber without bouncing out or missed entry

CRI = 100(St − 0.5Pt − 0.7Bt − 1.0Mt)
③

Rotational Test with Motor

Assesses: Full assembly completes 360° revolution under motor power without jamming or displacement

RSI = 100(St − 0.4Kt − 0.6Dt − 1.0Ft)

Conclusion

The rotating SLF trap successfully attracts, guides, and captures spotted lanternflies using low-power mechanical motion. All three subsystem tests exceeded the minimum threshold: GPI = 93.0, CRI = 92.5, RSI = 85.0. Small refinements including alignment correction and friction reduction drove most performance gains, confirming the design is robust and responsive to incremental optimization. Minor rotational instability did not prevent successful operation at prototype scale.

Future Suggestions

  • Scale to full version (1 m height, 1.07 m diameter) with 12V–24V high-torque DC gear motor
  • Develop and test under real vineyard conditions to evaluate long-term capture efficiency and durability
  • Explore weather-resistant materials and an automated attractant dispensing system
  • Improve outdoor reliability and passive operation
